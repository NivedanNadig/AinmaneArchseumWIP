Kodagu City

The kodavas of kodagu have a cultural way to build their houses, i.e. Ainmanes.
Photograph: Ashok S P

Kaimada/ ancestral Shrine

At the starting of every Ainmane there is a Kaimada, a Shrine Dedicated to the Ancestor of the clan/house.
Photograph:Shonali Madapa

Ubba and Bakka Pore

Through the shrine is a passageway for circumambulation around the altar which is called Ubba and Bakka Pore.
Photograph:Shonali Madapa

Kallbotti/ Stone Pillar

In the Middle of the patti/ courtyard a kallbotti is placed, a diya will be lit everyday after sunset and is kept on the pillar. These pillars are also used to tie bullocks.
Photograph:Shonali Madapa

The front kayyale/ verandah

Every Ainmane has a kayyale/ verandah in the front of the house where kids play and pictures of ancestors are displayed.
Photograph:Shonali Madapa

The Kanni Komba/ Sacred pillar

Kanni Komba is a Sacred piller which is installed in the sountwest corner of the mund.
Photograph:Shonali Madapa

Carved Pillar

The pillars in an Ainmane is carved, they also have hooks which can be used to hang anything. carvings on the pillar show sun,moon,trees etc, as the Kodavas are nature worshippers
Photograph:Shonali Madapa
